## Transfer Matrices Exercises

:::{exercise}

For _imaging_, the image height depends solely on the object height, not angle.
Given a transfer matrix:
$$
M = \begin{pmatrix} A & B \\ C & D \end{pmatrix}
$$

- Show that this requires $B=0$ and that magnification is given by $A$.
- Reconcile this observation with the following (incorrect) statement:
  > For a thin-lens, where the transfer matrix is given by $M = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -1/f & 1 \end{pmatrix}$, this would seem to imply magnification is independent of the focal length, $f$.
- Extend your reasoning to derive the thin-lens formula: $\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{d_{\mathrm{obj}}} + \frac{1}{d_{\mathrm{img}}}$.

:::
